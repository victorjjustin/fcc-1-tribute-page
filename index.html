Shigeru Miyamoto 宮本 茂

'The father of modern video games' -- Time Magazine

Highlights of Miyamoto

  • 1952 - Born in the town of Sonobe, located in Funai District, Kyoto Prefecture, Japan.
  • 1975 - Graduates from Kanazawa Municipal College of Industrial Arts with a degree in industrial desgn.
  • 1977 - Begins working as an apprentice in the planning department at Nintendo.
  • 1979 - Becomes the first staffed artist at Nintendo. Works on his first game Sheriff by helping create the artwork.
  • 1981 - Creates Donkey Kong for unsold arcade units. A first of its kind marking the beginning of the platforming genre.
  • 1984 - Promoted to General Manager of Nintendo EAD.
  • 1985 - Creates Super Mario Bros. for Nintendo's first home console the Famicom. Super Mario Bros. marks a transition from arcade to console. Miyamoto focuses on expanding gameplay over highscores. As of today, Mario is the best-selling video game franchise in history.
  • 1986 - Creates The Legend of Zelda, another ground breaking title for the Famicom and its newly released Disk System. The Legend of Zelda is based on his childhood experiences of adventure and uses a non-linear game design to allow for open world exploration.
  • 1990 - Produces Super Mario World. The first Mario installment for the Super Nintendo Entertainment System also known as the SNES.
  • 1991 - Produces The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past, the third entry in the series. The first to incorperate many common elements in later versions such as parallel worlds and the Master Sword.
  • 1993 - Produces and designs Star Fox, a rail-based shoot 'em up. Star Fox was the second 3D game Nintendo made and the first to use polygonal graphics with the Super FX graphic chip.
  • 1996 - Creates the revolutionary Super Mario 64 for the launch of the Nintendo 64 console. Miyamoto spent a year designing the game and two years producing it. Super Mario 64 was the first Nintendo game to feature 3D gameplay in an open world allowing the player complete freedom of movement.
  • 1998 - Produces The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time, the fifth game in the series and the first with 3D graphics. Widely regarded by many as the greatest video game of all time. It is the highest rated game of all time on Metacritic, a review aggregation website. Becomes the first person inducted into the Academy of Interactive Arts and Sciences' Hall of Fame
  • 2001 - Produces Luigi's Mansion for the launch of the GameCube. First revealed as a technical demo at Nintendo Space World 2000 to show off the GameCube's graphics. Nintendo later decided to turn Miyamoto's concept into a full game.
  • 2002 - Promoted to Senior Managing Director and Representative Director at Nintendo.
  • 2004 - Unveils The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess on stage at E3 dressed as Link with a sword and shield. Twilight Princess was among the launch titles of the Nintendo Wii and the first Zelda game to make use of motion controls.
  • 2005 - Produces Nintendogs, a real-time pet simulation game. Miyamoto based the game on his interactions with dogs. Nintendogs made use of the Nintendo DS's touch screen and microphone to simulate interaction. The title went on to become the second best selling game on the Nintendo DS.
  • 2006 - Named in TIME Asia's '60 Years of Asian Heroes' list alongside Mahatma Gandhi, Hayao Miyazaki, Mother Teresa, Bruce Lee and the Dalai Lama. Made a Chevalier (knight) of the French Ordre des Arts et des Lettres by the French Minister of Culture Renaud Donnedieu de Vabres.
  • 2007 - Appeared on Time Magazine's 100 Most Influential People of the Year list.
  • 2008 - Received the Lifetime Achievement Award at the Game Developers Choice Awards 'for a career that spans the creation of Super Mario Bros. and The Legend of Zelda, and Donkey Kong to the company's recent revolutionary systems, Nintendo DS and Wii'.
  • 2010 - Made a Fellow of BAFTA at the British Academy Video Games Awards.
  • 2012 - First interactive creator to be awarded the highest recognition in Spain, the Prince of Asturias Award, in the category of Communications and Humanities.
  • 2015 - Appointed the position of 'Creative Fellow' at Nintendo. In his new role, he provides feedback and guidance to game directors during development.
  • 2017 - Heavily involved in the design and construction of Super Nintendo World at Universal Studios in Japan.
  • 2019 - Awarded Japan's Person of Cultural Merit in 2019 in recognition for his contributions towards Japan's video game industry. He is the first person in the video game industry to receive the honor.
  • 2022 - Currently working as producer for an upcoming untitled Mario movie which is set to be released in North America on the 21st of December 2022.
